Winding Topology, Baryonic Charge, and Solitonic Phase Matter

Abstract

We construct and analyze the Skyrmion sector of Φ-Wave Theory, consisting of finite-energy, topologically stable phase configurations classified by winding number. These arise from mappings
\Phi : \mathbb{R}^3 \to SU(2)
compactified to
\Phi : S^3 \to S^3,
with topological charge given by the degree of the map. We derive the Skyrme-type energy functional from the Parent Φ-Wave Action, demonstrate stability through higher-order curvature terms, and interpret Skyrmions as particle-like excitations carrying conserved topological charge. We further analyze their structure, interactions, and role as a complementary particle sector to Hopfions, establishing winding topology as a fundamental mechanism for matter realization in Φ-Wave Theory.
