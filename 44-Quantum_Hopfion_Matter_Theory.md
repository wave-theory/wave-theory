Abstract

We develop the quantum theory of Hopfions, treating classical knotted phase configurations as quantum particle states within Φ-Wave Theory. By quantizing the moduli space of Hopfion solutions and incorporating functional fluctuations around topological backgrounds, we derive discrete energy spectra, spin-like quantum numbers, and interaction structures. We show that Hopfions form a natural class of particle-like excitations whose properties arise from topological invariants, linking structure, and phase geometry. This establishes a concrete realization of matter as quantized phase topology.
