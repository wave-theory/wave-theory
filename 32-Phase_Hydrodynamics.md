Abstract

We derive hydrodynamics as an emergent collective limit of Φ-Wave Theory. By interpreting gradients of the phase field as velocity fields and coarse-graining over microscopic phase structure, we show that classical fluid equations—including continuity, Euler, and Navier–Stokes-like dynamics—arise naturally from the nonlinear phase wave equations. We identify density, velocity, vorticity, and pressure as emergent quantities derived from phase geometry and curvature. This establishes fluids as macroscopic manifestations of phase flow, unifying fluid dynamics with field theory and topology.
