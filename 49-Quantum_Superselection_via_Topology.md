Emergence of Superselection Rules from Phase Configuration Structure

Abstract

We derive quantum superselection rules in Φ-Wave Theory as a direct consequence of the topological and admissibility structure of the phase configuration space. By analyzing the decomposition
\mathcal{C}_{\mathrm{phys}} = \bigsqcup_\alpha \mathcal{C}_\alpha
into disconnected sectors labeled by topological invariants and cohomology classes, we show that quantum states belonging to distinct sectors cannot interfere unless admissible finite-action transitions exist between them. This leads to a natural decomposition of the Hilbert space into superselection sectors, within which coherent superposition is allowed, but across which it is forbidden. We demonstrate that conserved quantities such as charge, topological number, and certain gauge invariants arise as labels of these sectors. This establishes superselection not as an additional postulate, but as a necessary consequence of phase topology.
