Abstract

We present a structured embedding attempt of the Standard Model within Φ-Wave Theory. The goal is to determine whether the gauge group, charge structure, chirality, and matter sectors of the Standard Model can arise from phase geometry, topological defects, bundle structure, and admissibility constraints. We propose that the physical gauge structure emerges from phase transport on a manifold with local symmetry group
G_{\mathrm{SM}} = \frac{SU(3)\times SU(2)\times U(1)}{\Gamma},
with \Gamma a discrete quotient enforcing the observed charge lattice. We show how gauge bosons arise as quantum curvature modes of the phase connection, how charge assignments can be encoded in the quotient structure and cohomological lattice, and how chiral matter may arise from defect-localized zero modes. We also identify the main unresolved problems: full chiral family replication, realistic Yukawa structure, anomaly cancellation in complete generality, and exact mass generation. This paper is therefore an explicit Standard Model embedding program, not yet a final derivation.
