Abstract

We construct the Quantum Charge Lattice in Φ-Wave Theory, showing that electric and generalized gauge charges arise as elements of a discrete lattice determined by phase topology, cohomology, and global quotient structure \Gamma. Extending the integer winding result of abelian phase theory, we demonstrate that physically realized charges form a lattice embedded in the cohomology group of the phase bundle. We further show that fractional charges naturally emerge when the global phase symmetry is reduced by a discrete quotient G/\Gamma. This framework provides a unified topological origin of charge quantization, fractionalization, and charge conservation, and establishes the mathematical structure necessary for Standard Model–like charge assignments.
