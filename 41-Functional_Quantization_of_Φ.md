Abstract

We develop the functional quantization of the phase field \Phi, constructing a path-integral formulation over the space of admissible phase configurations. We show that quantum behavior emerges as a statistical and interference structure over classical phase histories, constrained by admissibility and global consistency. The resulting formulation reproduces quantum field theory in appropriate limits while preserving the primacy of phase as the fundamental ontological object. This establishes quantum mechanics as an emergent statistical theory of phase evolution rather than a fundamental postulate.
