Abstract

We formulate Φ-Wave Theory in the language of fiber bundles, establishing that the phase field
\Phi : M \to \mathcal{M}
naturally induces a principal fiber bundle structure over spacetime. Gauge fields arise as connections on this bundle, curvature corresponds to phase transport holonomy, and topological sectors are classified by bundle invariants. We demonstrate that all previously derived structures—gauge fields, charge quantization, and particle topology—are unified within a single bundle-theoretic framework. This paper provides the geometric backbone linking Φ-Wave Theory to modern differential geometry and gauge theory.
