Abstract

We construct Quantum Phase Electrodynamics (Φ-QED) as the abelian specialization of quantum phase connection dynamics. In this framework, the electromagnetic field arises as the quantized curvature of a U(1) phase connection, and electric charge emerges as a topological index associated with phase winding. We derive the full quantum dynamics of the abelian connection coupled to phase matter, recover the standard structure of QED in the appropriate limit, and reinterpret photons as curvature quanta of phase transport. We further show that gauge invariance, charge conservation, and electromagnetic interactions follow directly from phase covariance and admissibility. This establishes electromagnetism as a fundamental manifestation of quantum phase geometry.
