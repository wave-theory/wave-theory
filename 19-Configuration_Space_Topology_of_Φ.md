Abstract

We construct and analyze the topology of the configuration space
\mathcal{C} = \{\Phi : M \to \mathcal{M}\}

We show that this infinite-dimensional space possesses nontrivial topological structure, including disconnected components labeled by homotopy classes and nontrivial fundamental group structure governing defect exchange and statistics. We demonstrate that quantum sector decomposition, superselection rules, and particle statistics arise from the topology of \mathcal{C}, rather than from independent postulates. This paper establishes configuration space topology as the origin of quantum structure and statistical behavior in Φ-Wave Theory.
