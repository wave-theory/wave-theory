Abstract

We develop a theory of causality in Φ-Wave Theory, reconciling global phase configuration structure with local relativistic causal constraints. We show that while quantum states are defined over globally admissible phase configurations, dynamical propagation is governed by local phase curvature and connection structure, yielding an emergent light-cone structure. We demonstrate that apparent quantum nonlocality arises from global constraints on admissible phase histories rather than superluminal signaling. Measurement updates are reinterpreted as global admissibility restrictions consistent with causal propagation. This establishes causality as an emergent geometric constraint on phase evolution rather than a primitive postulate.
