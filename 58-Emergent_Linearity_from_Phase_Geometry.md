Linear Quantum Structure as an Effective Representation of Nonlinear Phase Dynamics

Abstract

We demonstrate that the apparent linearity of quantum mechanics emerges from an underlying nonlinear geometric phase theory when restricted to admissible configuration space and coarse-grained observables. Starting from the nonlinear Parent Φ-Wave Action, we show that the functional representation of phase configurations yields a linear Hilbert space structure due to superposition of admissible histories, orthogonality of sector decompositions, and measurement-induced admissibility restriction. We derive the effective linear evolution of wavefunctionals, identify the conditions under which linearity holds, and characterize regimes where deviations from linearity may occur. This establishes linear quantum mechanics as an emergent, not fundamental, structure.
