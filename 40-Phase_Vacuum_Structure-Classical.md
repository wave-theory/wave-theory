Abstract

We develop the classical theory of vacuum structure in Φ-Wave Theory, defining vacuum states as globally admissible, energy-minimizing phase configurations. We show that the vacuum is not necessarily unique but may form a degenerate manifold of phase ground states determined by the phase potential V(\Phi), topology, and admissibility constraints. We analyze vacuum stability, classify vacuum sectors, and demonstrate how vacuum structure governs particle formation, symmetry breaking, and cosmological dynamics. This establishes the vacuum as a geometric and topological object rather than an empty background.
