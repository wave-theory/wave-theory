Φ-WAVE THEORY

Paper 53 — Quantum Phase Renormalization

Scale Dependence, Effective Actions, and Flow in Phase Geometry

⸻

Abstract

We develop the theory of renormalization in Φ-Wave Theory, showing that scale dependence arises from integrating over phase fluctuations in the space of admissible configurations. We construct the effective action via functional integration, derive renormalization group (RG) flow equations for phase couplings, and demonstrate how classical phase geometry is modified at different energy scales. We show that divergences correspond to unresolved short-scale phase structure and that renormalization corresponds to coarse-graining over phase configurations. This establishes a Phase-native formulation of quantum field renormalization and provides the foundation for high-energy consistency, scaling behavior, and Standard Model embedding.

3. Phase Field Decomposition

\boxed{

* \Phi_<: low-energy modes (|k| < \Lambda)

4. Effective Action Definition

Integrate out high-energy modes:

\boxed{
e^{iS_{\mathrm{eff}}[\Phi_<]/\hbar}
=
\int \mathcal{D}\Phi_>\;
e^{iS[\Phi_< + \Phi_>]/\hbar}
}

This defines the effective phase action.
