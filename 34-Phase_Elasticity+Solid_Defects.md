Abstract

We develop a theory of solid-state behavior within Φ-Wave Theory by identifying crystalline order as spontaneously organized phase configurations. Elasticity emerges from deviations of phase structure from equilibrium configurations, while solid defects—dislocations, disclinations, and grain boundaries—are interpreted as topological defects in the phase field. We derive elastic energy functionals from the Parent Φ-Wave Action, establish stress–strain relations in terms of phase gradients, and show that defect dynamics follow from admissibility and phase curvature. This unifies solid mechanics with topology and field theory.
