Abstract

We develop the theory of quantum superposition across topological sectors in Φ-Wave Theory. Using the Hilbert space decomposition
\mathcal{H}_\Phi = \bigoplus_\alpha \mathcal{H}_\alpha,
we analyze when and how quantum states can form coherent superpositions of configurations with distinct topological invariants. We show that superposition is constrained by admissibility, sector connectivity, and dynamical accessibility. In cases where sectors are connected via admissible transitions, interference between topological classes is possible; otherwise, superselection rules emerge. This establishes quantum topology as a dynamical—not purely kinematic—feature of Φ.
